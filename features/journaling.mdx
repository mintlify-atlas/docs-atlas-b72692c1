## Calendar-Based Entry Management

Reflect AI uses a calendar view as the foundation for your journaling practice. Each day becomes a dedicated space for your thoughts, making it easy to see your writing history at a glance.

### Navigating Your Journal

**Monthly Calendar View**
- Days with entries are visually highlighted and show mood indicators
- See preview snippets of your entries directly on the calendar
- Navigate between months using the arrow buttons or jump to today instantly

**Year View**
- Browse all years with journal entries
- Drill down into specific months to see your writing patterns
- Quickly navigate to any date to review or continue writing

<Tip>
Use the **Today** button in the sidebar to quickly jump to the current date, perfect for maintaining your daily writing habit.
</Tip>

## Writing Your Entries

### Getting Started

Click any date on the calendar to open the editor. You'll see:
- A full-screen writing space with minimal distractions
- The current date displayed prominently
- Real-time word count tracking
- Auto-save functionality (entries save when you click Save)

### Quick Nudges Feature

When starting a new entry, you can use **Quick Nudges** to capture moments throughout your day:

1. Add short notes like "morning coffee," "team meeting went well," or "felt anxious about presentation"
2. Collect multiple nudges as your day unfolds
3. Click **Generate Entry from Nudges** to have AI expand your notes into a cohesive journal entry
4. Edit and personalize the generated text before saving

<Note>
Quick Nudges require a Groq API key. If you prefer to write freely, click "Skip" to access the regular text editor.
</Note>

## Enhancing Your Entries

### Tags

Organize entries with custom tags:
- Click the **+ tag** button to add labels like "work," "personal," "travel"
- Maximum 10 tags per entry, each up to 30 characters
- Use tags to identify themes and patterns over time

### Photos

Attach visual memories to your journal entries:
- Click the photo icon to upload images
- Add up to 10 photos per entry
- Photos are stored as base64 data in your local journal file

<Warning>
Photos increase your journal file size. Consider exporting and backing up regularly if you add many images.
</Warning>

## Word Count Tracking

Reflect AI tracks your writing volume:
- Live word count displays in the editor as you type
- Entry word counts are saved with each journal entry
- View average words per entry in your Analytics dashboard
- Track writing trends over time

## Voice Journaling

Speak your thoughts instead of typing (browser support required):

1. Click the microphone icon in the editor
2. Grant microphone permissions when prompted
3. Speak your journal entry naturally
4. Your speech is transcribed directly into the text editor

<Info>
Voice journaling works in Chrome, Edge, and Safari. The feature uses your browser's built-in speech recognition—no audio is sent to external servers.
</Info>

## Auto-Save and Data Protection

### Saving Your Work

- Click **Save** to store your entry
- Entries are written atomically with backup protection
- You'll be warned if you try to leave with unsaved changes
- Each save includes an automatic sentiment analysis

### Entry Management

**Editing Entries**: Click any calendar date to reopen and modify past entries

**Deleting Entries**: 
- Click the trash icon in the editor
- Confirm deletion (this action cannot be undone)
- Empty entries (no text or photos) are automatically deleted on save

## Writing Enhancement Tools

### Polish Writing

Use AI to refine your writing:
1. Write your entry naturally
2. Click **Polish Writing**
3. AI rewrites your text for clarity and flow
4. Use **Undo** if you prefer your original version

### Get Suggestions

Receive AI-powered writing prompts:
- Click **Get Suggestions** while writing
- AI analyzes your current text and offers:
  - Questions to explore deeper
  - Related themes to consider
  - Alternative perspectives

<Note>
Both polish and suggestions features require a Groq API key configured in your `.env` file.
</Note>

## Privacy First

All journal entries are stored locally in `journal_data.json`:
- No cloud uploads or external databases
- Your writing never leaves your device
- AI features (when used) only send the specific text you're editing
- Export your data anytime to maintain complete ownership
