## Understanding Streaks

Your **streak** is the number of consecutive days you've written journal entries. Reflect AI tracks your streak automatically, giving you a clear metric for consistency.

### How Streaks Are Calculated

**Current Streak**:
- Counts consecutive days with at least one journal entry
- Must include either today or yesterday to be "active"
- Breaks automatically if you miss two days in a row

**Longest Streak**:
- Your all-time record for consecutive journaling days
- Preserved even if your current streak breaks
- Serves as a personal best to aim for

<Note>
You have until midnight to maintain your streak. Streaks are based on dates, not 24-hour periods from your last entry.
</Note>

## Streak Alerts

### At-Risk Warning

When your streak is in danger, Reflect AI shows an urgent alert:

```
⏰ Only 6h left to save your 14-day streak!
```

**Triggers**:
- You haven't journaled today
- Less than 6 hours remain until midnight
- You have an active streak of at least 1 day

### Gentle Reminders

Before the urgent alert, you'll see friendly reminders:

```
Don't forget to write today! 12h remaining.
```

**Triggers**:
- You haven't journaled today
- 6-12 hours remain until midnight
- You have an active streak

<Tip>
Set aside a consistent time each day (morning or evening) to journal. Routine reduces the chance you'll forget and break your streak.
</Tip>

## Weekly Goals

Set and track weekly journaling targets.

### Default Goal

Reflect AI sets a default goal of **5 entries per week**—a sustainable pace that maintains consistency without overwhelming you.

### Progress Tracking

**Visual Progress Bar**:
- Green bar fills as you complete entries
- Shows current count / target (e.g., "3/5 days")
- Turns gold when you hit your weekly goal

**What Counts**:
- Any entry written within the last 7 days
- Entries must have text (empty entries don't count)
- Editing an existing entry doesn't add to the count

<Check>
Completing your weekly goal triggers a celebration toast: "🎉 Weekly goal achieved! Great consistency!"
</Check>

## Strategies for Building Streaks

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Start with a micro-habit">
    Don't aim for 1000-word entries daily. Start with 50-100 words. It's better to write a little every day than to write novels inconsistently.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Set a daily reminder">
    Use your phone or calendar to remind you at the same time each day. Consistency thrives on routine.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use the Quick Nudges feature">
    On busy days, jot down quick nudges throughout the day, then generate a full entry later. This lowers the activation energy for journaling.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Write early in the day">
    Don't leave journaling until bedtime. You're more likely to forget or be too tired. Morning or lunch journaling protects your streak.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Track your streak publicly (optional)">
    Tell a friend or post about it on social media. External accountability can boost commitment.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Focus on the streak, not perfection">
    Some days your entry will be mediocre. That's okay! The habit of showing up matters more than every entry being profound.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What to Do If You Break Your Streak

### It's Normal

Life happens. Most people break streaks multiple times before building a long-lasting habit.

### Recovery Strategy

1. **Don't catastrophize**: One missed day doesn't erase your progress
2. **Restart immediately**: Write today, right now
3. **Analyze what happened**: Was it a schedule issue? Forgot? Too busy?
4. **Adjust your routine**: Build in safeguards to prevent the same issue
5. **Focus on longest streak**: Your personal record is still there to beat

<Note>
Your longest streak is preserved. Use it as motivation—you've done it before, you can do it again (and go further!).
</Note>

### The Fresh Start Effect

Many users find renewed motivation after a break. Use it as a chance to:
- Recommit with fresh energy
- Refine your journaling time and approach
- Aim for your next milestone with lessons learned
